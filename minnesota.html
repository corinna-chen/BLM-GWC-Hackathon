Civil unrest in Minneapolis quickly spread to other cities and inspired a global protest movement against police brutaility and racial inequality. Four days after killing George Floyd by kneeling on his neck during an arrest, former Minneapolis police officer Derek Chauvin was charged with third-degree murder. This was later updated to second-degree murder. The three other police officers present at Floyd's arrest have also been charged with aiding and abetting second-degree murder. Minneapolis lawmakers vowed to disband the city's police department less than two weeks after Floyd's death.
